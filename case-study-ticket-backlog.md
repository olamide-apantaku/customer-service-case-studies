# Case Study: Clearing a Ticket Backlog

**Industry:** Telecom
**Role:** Customer Service Representative
**Scenario:** High volume ticket backlog during 
peak period

## Background
During a major MTN Nigeria network outage, 
inbound customer contacts increased by over 
200% within 24 hours. The support team was 
overwhelmed with complaints, enquiries and 
escalations resulting in a significant ticket 
backlog that was threatening SLA compliance 
and customer satisfaction scores.

## Challenge
- Ticket volume increased by 200%+ overnight
- SLA targets at risk of being breached
- Customer frustration levels extremely high
- Team stretched beyond normal capacity
- No additional agents available immediately

## Actions Taken

**1. Immediate Triage**
- Categorised all open tickets by priority level
- Separated urgent and high priority tickets 
  from low and medium priority
- Created a clear action list starting with 
  most critical tickets first
- Flagged tickets at risk of SLA breach 
  to Team Lead immediately

**2. Streamlining Response Process**
- Created templated responses for the most 
  common outage related enquiries
- Used macros in CRM to respond to multiple 
  similar tickets simultaneously
- Reduced average handle time by focusing 
  on clear and concise communication
- Avoided unnecessary back and forth by 
  providing complete information upfront

**3. Proactive Customer Communication**
- Sent proactive updates to all affected 
  customers with expected resolution timeline
- Reduced repeat contacts by keeping customers 
  informed at every stage
- Acknowledged all tickets within 1 hour 
  even if full resolution was not yet available
- Used empathy statements to manage 
  customer frustration

**4. Team Coordination**
- Communicated regularly with Team Lead 
  on backlog progress
- Flagged complex cases for specialist handling
- Shared templated responses with colleagues 
  to speed up team wide response time
- Prioritised collaborative resolution of 
  high priority tickets

## Result

| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ticket Backlog | 200%+ increase | Cleared within 48 hours | Significant |
| SLA Compliance | At risk | Maintained | 95-100% |
| Customer Contacts | High repeat contacts | Reduced | Proactive updates |
| Team Response Time | Slow | Improved | Templates adopted |

**Backlog cleared within 48 hours**

## Key Learnings
- Immediate triage is critical when volume spikes —
  prioritise before responding
- Templated responses maintain quality while 
  dramatically reducing handle time
- Proactive communication reduces repeat contacts 
  and manages customer expectations
- Team coordination during high volume periods 
  is as important as individual performance
- Documenting the process helps the team prepare 
  better for future peak periods

## Skills Demonstrated
- Ticket prioritisation and triage
- SLA management under pressure
- Proactive customer communication
- CRM efficiency and macro usage
- Team coordination and collaboration
- Operational efficiency
